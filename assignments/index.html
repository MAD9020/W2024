# MAD9020 Deliverables

Every week there will be work for you to complete inside or outside of class. Some exercises will be larger than others. There will be 5 exercises (10%), midterm (30%), mini project (15%), major project (35%), 2 quizzes (10%).

# Exercises 10%


Week 1 - User goals and business goals

Due: Wednesday January 10th (end of class)

In Week 1, we delve into understanding and defining the user and business goals for three selected products/services/sites, focusing on their full website and mobile site/app functionalities.

View Details

Week 2 - Usability testing

Due: Wednesday January 17th (end of class)

In Week 2, we engage in a hands-on usability test by creating origami animals, focusing on the user experience and the effectiveness of the origami instructions, with roles divided among group members for a comprehensive evaluation.

View Details

Week 3 - Heuristic Evaluation

Due: Wednesday January 24th (end of class)

In Week 3, we'll conduct a heuristic evaluation in pairs on an application of your choice, identifying and analyzing elements that either adhere to or violate established usability heuristics, with the aim of providing objective, actionable feedback.

View Details

Week 9 - Application Platform Conversion

Due: Wednesday March 6th (end of day)

In Week 9, working in pairs, we'll embark on a detailed investigation of the main mobile phone platforms, analyzing their application design guidelines to understand and document key requirements and unique features specific to each platform.

View Details

Week 10 - Compare input methods

Due: Wednesday March 13th (end of class)

In Week 10, groups of up to three will engage in a comparative analysis of different input methods for specific data points on a touch-screen mobile device, focusing on efficiency, error prevention, and user guidance, to determine the most effective input method for each scenario.

View Details

# Midterm Project 30%


Part 1: Information architecture

Due: Wednesday January 31st (end of class)

Apply your knowledge from this week's lesson to create a comprehensive information architecture for Winterlude, incorporating specific content elements and ensuring a user-friendly structure that enhances the overall experience.

View Details

Part 2: Sketching and iterating wireframes

Due: Wednesday February 14th (end of class)

Engage in a creative brainstorming session by sketching wireframes for a Winterlude app, incorporating different navigation models and iterating based on peer feedback to refine your concepts and enhance user interaction and navigation.

View Details

Part 3: Presenting your concepts

Due: Wednesday February 21st (end of class)

Prepare and deliver an in-class presentation of your app concept for Winterlude, focusing on wireframe design, user interface principles, and navigation models, with an emphasis on clear communication, content quality, and the overall coherence of your proposed concept.

View Details

# Mini Project 15%


Checkout sequence for flower delivery

Due: Wednesday April 3rd (start of class)

Design a comprehensive and user-friendly checkout sequence for a flower delivery service, creating detailed wireframes in Figma to showcase each step of the process, ensuring logical layout, clear communication, and adherence to platform design guidelines.

View Details

# Final Project 35%


Grocery store self-checkout

Due: Wednesday April 17th (end of class)

For your final project, you will design a new touch screen interface for a grocery store self-checkout system, involving field research to identify a key usability issue, creating an interactive prototype to address this problem, and presenting your solution in class, all while adhering to fundamental UI and UX design principles.

View Details
