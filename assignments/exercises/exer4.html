# Week 9 - Application Platform Conversion Exercise

# Overview

This exercise focuses on developing students' abilities to convert app designs from one platform to another, adhering closely to the specific design guidelines of the target platform. Through this exercise, performed in predetermined pairs, students will work on converting specified applications from Android to iOS and vice versa. The process demands a thorough understanding of Apple's Human Interface Guidelines and Google's Material Design, fostering skills in maintaining an app's core functionality and identity when transitioning between platforms.

# Objectives

  • Understand platform-specific design guidelines: Master the distinct design principles and guidelines of Apple's Human Interface Guidelines and Google's Material Design.
  • Acquire cross-platform conversion expertise: Learn how to adapt an app's design for another platform, ensuring usability and fidelity to the platform's conventions.
  • Enhance proficiency in Figma: Utilize Figma to demonstrate adeptness in converting app designs between platforms, utilizing available resources for accuracy and efficiency.

# Instructions

  1. Group Formation: The exercise will be completed in groups of 2.

  2. Provided Applications: Each group will work on converting two pre-selected applications:

From Android to iOS From iOS to Android

  1. Leverage Figma Community Resources:

    • To aid in your design process, students are encouraged to download and install the iOS Design Library and Templates (opens new window) and the Material Design Kit 2 (opens new window) from the Figma Community. These resources provide a wealth of components and design patterns that align with the respective design guidelines, facilitating a more efficient and accurate conversion process.

  2. Study Design Guidelines:

    • Apple’s Human Interface Guidelines: developer.apple.com/design/human-interface-guidelines/ios/ (opens new window)
    • Google’s Material Design: m3.material.io (opens new window) Focus on comprehending the core principles, component usage, and interaction paradigms unique to each platform.

  3. Convert the Applications:

    • Utilize Figma to create high-fidelity prototypes showcasing the conversion of the provided applications. Ensure that your designs reflect the target platform's aesthetic, usability, and interaction standards.
    • Address key aspects such as navigation, iconography, typography, and color schemes, emphasizing adherence to platform-specific guidelines.
    • Place the original screens (image) next to the converted wireframes.
    • Add annotations to the converted wireframes highlighting the changes / differences in the platform standards.

# Deliverable

  • Figma Prototype: A link to your Figma project with the converted application designs with the annotated changes.

# Submission

  • Deadline: Monday, March 6th, by end of day
  • Upload the Figma prototype link to the BS LMS under Activities > Assignments > Week 9 In-class: Application Platform Conversion.

# Evaluation

  • Compliance with the target platform's design guidelines.
  • The quality of functionality and user experience in the converted design.
  • The depth and insightfulness of the conversion report.
  • Skillful use of Figma and incorporation of community resources in the prototypes.

# Note

  • The focus is on design principles and their application across platforms.
  • Collaboration within the group is essential for a successful outcome, encouraging a division of labor based on strengths and interests.
