# Week 2 - Usability test

# Details

# For this exercise, we will be making origami animals, and evaluating the 'usability' of the experience.

  • Origami Giraffe Instructions (opens new window)
  • Origami Elephant Instructions (opens new window)
  • Origami Crane Instructions (opens new window)

# We will organize into groups of 3. Each group will appoint:

  • a moderator
  • a participant
  • a note-taker

After each round, members will switch roles.

# Preparation

  • Test participant opens one of the Origami instructions
  • Prepare a sheet of square paper
  • Moderator Scrip (opens new window)
  • Note-taker Notes (opens new window)

# Making the Origami

Each participant will have 15 minutes to complete the origami animal.

# Using the Origami Instructions

Each participant will have 15 minutes to complete the assigned tasks with one of the Origami Instructions.

  • During that time:
    • the moderator gives simple instructions. They tell the participant that we are evaluating the origami instructions, and want to see how easy they are to follow. The moderator should ask questions to understand what the participant is experiencing, but can not offer help.
    • the participant tries their best to create the origami animal. They can re-start with a new piece of paper if needed, but can not ask for help, look at other participants, or use any additional tools/help online.
    • the note-taker is documenting the process. Observe things like:
      • Successes/failures
      • Confusion
      • Frustration
      • Emotional reactions
      • Specific steps in the instructions where these occur
  • After 15 minutes, we will stop, and together discuss how it went. Observers share their notes, participants respond with anything else that wasn't documented.
  • Then, we change roles, and do the exercise a second time, with a different set of task list and origami instructions, and discuss that.

# Deliverable for this exercise:

  • The observer notes from your group. Each group will submit three sets of observer notes. (Take a photo/scan of the page of notes).
  • Filename: group_usabilityTesting.docx (or another file extension)
